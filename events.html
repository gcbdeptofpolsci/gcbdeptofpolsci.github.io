Events organised by Department Of Political Science

Event Briefings

27 July 2024

A seminar on Budget 2024 was organized under the aegis of the Department of Political Science and the Department of Commerce at Atal Bihari Vajpayee Government College, Bangana. The program was presided over by Professor Sikandar Negi, Head of the Department of Political Science. The stage was conducted by General Secretary Pariksha of Political Science. The guest of the program was Professor Anil Sharma, Head of the Department of Commerce. The key speakers of the seminar were Professor Krishna Chand, Professor Kamlesh Mahajan Department of Commerce, Professor Nikita Gupta Department of Economics, Nikhil and Vasu Sharma. The topic of the special seminar was Budget 2024, what is budget, main documents of Indian budget, how many types of budget are there, importance and process of budget, when does budget making start? The key speakers of the seminar were BA first year students Vasu Sharma and Nikhil Bagoria. Nikhil and Vasu Sharma told that the form in which a statement of the income and expenditure of the government is collected is called budget. The annual financial statement of the estimates of income and expenditure of the previous year is presented in the budget. Article 112 of the Indian Constitution talks about budget making. Five types of budgets are used in India, general budget, performance budget, zero-base budget, outcome budget, and gender budget. The budget making process in India is started in the month of August-September. A budget circular is issued for this. The Budget Division under the Finance Department issues a budget circular at the end of August or the beginning of September. All the works of the government are determined by the budget. The arrangement of funds for all the schemes run by the government is done through the budget only. The government cannot spend even a rupee without a budget. New schemes are announced every year in the budget and amendments are made in the tax, which directly affects the public. All the provisions made in the budget cannot be implemented until it is passed by the Parliament. On this occasion, Professor Sikandar Negi, Head of the Department of Political Science, General Secretary Examination, Cashier Tikshan and Media Incharge Rohit, Kushal Rana etc. were present.
